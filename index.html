What We do

We offer pro-bono group counseling, individual counseling, and psycho-educational sessions for community members affected by racial & community injustice.

Who We Are

Ivette Acevedo-Weatherholtz, MA, LPC

Ivette Acevedo-Weatherholtz was born in Mexico and immigrated to Washington State with her family when she was 10 years old. Ivette is a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) in the state of Missouri who specializes in developmental and complex trauma, attachment, and social-emotional learning in youth ages 2-21. She also often facilitates trauma-informed care and attachment trainings to educators and parents. Ivette is a bilingual therapist and has significant experience in providing somatic-based interventions and EMDR therapy to refugee and immigrant youth.

Jon-Pierre Mitchom, MA, LPC

Jon-Pierre Mitchom resided in East Saint Louis during his early childhood years and transitioned with his family to living in South St. Louis City by early elementary where he became a participant in the desegregation school busing program. From an early age, he has experienced and become acquainted with the challenges, impact and nuances of oppression and discrimination. Jon-Pierre is now a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) specializing in treating chronic stress and anxiety, depression, race-based trauma, and marriage and family challenges for adolescents and adults. He works for the St. Louis County Family Courts as an anger management instructor, a school counselor in the Parkway School District, and has a private practice located in South St. Louis City. He facilitates trainings and workshops on Social Justice/Dismantling Racism and Restorative Practices for adult staff and students within the K-12 educational system and in the community at large. Jon-Pierre’s community work has been featured on the St. Louis Public radio show We Live Here. He has been a presenter at the Ferguson Readings on Race Community Group and the Ferguson Human Rights Commission. As a mental health activist, Jon-Pierre looks forward to continuing to dismantle systemic oppression/discrimination through training and providing counseling support.

Jill Harding, MA, LPC, NCC

Jill Harding has lived in St. Louis since 2011. She is a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) in the state of Missouri who specializes in grief and loss, anxiety, depression, attachment issues, and trauma. She currently works as a grief counselor at Heartland Hospice and also owns a private practice called Magnolia Counseling Services located in South St. Louis City. She has been working with counseling clients in a variety of settings for over 5 years. Jill is also involved with local activist group Faith For Justice and works closely with the St. Louis Action Council to pursue social and political justice primarily at the local and state levels. Jill seeks to help emotionally equip other activists and those affected by racial and community injustice as they fight for public justice in an often hostile context and as they work towards personal healing.
